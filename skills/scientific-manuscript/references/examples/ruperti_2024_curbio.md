# Ruperti et al., 2024, Current Biology — Annotated Examples

**Paper**: Molecular profiling of sponge deflation reveals an ancient relaxant-inflammatory response
**DOI**: 10.1016/j.cub.2023.12.021
**Key topic**: Sponge movement driven by actomyosin relaxation, not contraction; conserved relaxant-inflammatory module

---

## Abstract: Opening Hook

**Excerpt**:
> A hallmark of animals is the coordination of whole-body movement. Neurons and muscles are central to this, yet coordinated movements also exist in sponges that lack these cell types.

**Why this works**:
- Opens with universal principle (animal movement)
- Immediately introduces the paradox (coordination without neurons/muscles)
- Creates intellectual tension in just two sentences
- Accessible to non-specialists

**Principle**: Start with a universal principle, then introduce the exception or paradox your work addresses.

---

## Abstract: Comprehensive Results

**Excerpt**:
> We combine live 3D optical coherence microscopy, pharmacology, and functional proteomics to elucidate the sequence and detail of shape changes, the tissues and molecular physiology involved, and the control of these movements. Morphometric analysis and targeted perturbation suggest that the movement is driven by the relaxation of actomyosin stress fibers in epithelial canal cells, which leads to whole-body deflation via collapse of the incurrent and expansion of the excurrent canal system.

**Why this works**:
- Lists methods concisely (establishes technical rigor)
- States central finding clearly (relaxation, not contraction)
- Includes mechanistic detail (which cells, what happens)
- Contradicts existing model explicitly ("contrary to current models" appears elsewhere)

**Principle**: When your findings overturn conventional wisdom, state the new model clearly and specifically.

---

## Abstract: Significance Statement

**Excerpt**:
> These results suggest the conservation of an ancient relaxant-inflammatory response of perturbed fluid-carrying systems in animals and offer a possible mechanism for whole-body coordination through diffusible paracrine signals and mechanotransduction.

**Why this works**:
- Connects specific finding to deep evolutionary conservation
- Proposes general mechanism beyond the specific system
- Uses appropriately hedged language ("suggest," "possible")
- Ends with the broad conceptual advance

**Principle**: End abstracts with the evolutionary or mechanistic principle, not just the observation.

## Introduction: The Gap Statement

**Excerpt**:
> In recent years, several studies have addressed the cellular and molecular underpinnings of sponge deflation. Current models propose cellular contraction of incurrent pinacocytes. Supporting this, pinacocytes express genes involved in smooth and non-muscle actomyosin contractility and contain actin stress fibers that traverse the cytoplasm, connecting neighboring cells via mixed focal adhesion/adherens-like junctions. Treatment with selected pharmacological compounds has indicated involvement of myosin light-chain kinase (MYLK), cGMP, and nitric oxide (NO) signaling. Yet the exact cellular processes and physiological changes driving and accompanying these processes have largely remained obscure.

**Why this works**:
- Summarizes prior work fairly
- Shows what the field thinks (contraction model)
- Lists supporting evidence for that model
- Clear pivot: "Yet... have largely remained obscure"
- Gap is specific: "exact cellular processes"

**Rhetorical moves**:
1. Acknowledge prior work
2. State current model
3. List supporting evidence (shows you know the field)
4. Pivot to what remains unknown

**Principle**: Present the existing model generously before revealing its limitations.

## Results: Challenging the Paradigm

**Excerpt**:
> These observations strongly suggested that S. lacustris deflation is initiated by a reduction of actomyosin crossbridge cycling through MLCP activation, which equals actomyosin relaxation—either via activation of Akt kinase/NO/PKG or PKA or via inhibition of RhoA/ROCK and subsequent dephosphorylation of the myosin light chain (MYL).

**Why this works**:
- Explicitly states what the data suggest
- Uses clear mechanistic language
- Integrates multiple lines of evidence
- Names the pathway components specifically

**Principle**: When presenting data that challenges existing models, be explicit about what your data show and the mechanistic implications.

## Results: Section Heading (Conclusory)

**Excerpt**:
> "An Akt kinase/NO/PKG/PKA pathway triggers tension release in epithelial stress fibers"

**Why this works**:
- States the conclusion, not the method
- Names the pathway specifically
- Tells the reader what they'll learn
- Could stand alone as a finding

**Contrast with weak alternative**: "Pharmacological analysis of actomyosin regulation"

**Principle**: Section headings should be mini-abstracts of the section's findings.

## Results: Quantitative Specificity

**Excerpt**:
> Overnight time-lapse OCM imaging captured endogenous deflations with a reduction in sponge volume of approximately 40%. Closure of ostia and collapse of the tent and incurrent canals resulted in near total loss (~96%) of incurrent volume, with 108% volume and 36% surface area increase in the excurrent system.

**Why this works**:
- Specific percentages throughout
- Distinguishes different measurements (volume vs. surface area)
- Includes the direction of change (loss vs. increase)
- Precise language ("near total loss")

**Principle**: Replace qualitative descriptions with quantitative measurements wherever possible.

## Discussion: Connecting to Broader Biology

**Excerpt**:
> The detection and evolutionary traceability of such a "relaxant-inflammatory" module suggests the conservation of an ancient response against disturbances in normal, laminar flow as part of metazoan epithelia.

**Why this works**:
- Names the concept clearly ("relaxant-inflammatory module")
- States the evolutionary implication
- Proposes a functional explanation (response to flow disturbance)
- Connects to broader biology (metazoan epithelia)

**Principle**: Name your conceptual advances explicitly — this helps readers remember and cite your work.

## Methods Integration in Results

**Excerpt**:
> We reasoned that near-infrared optical coherence microscopy (OCM) would provide strong, label-free contrast between the tissue and water-filled cavities while ensuring overall low phototoxicity, a large field of view, and fast imaging speed. We therefore customized an OCM system for 3D morphological imaging of 8-day-old juvenile S. lacustris during deflation.

**Why this works**:
- Explains the rationale for method choice
- Lists specific advantages (contrast, phototoxicity, field of view, speed)
- Brief but sufficient detail
- Appears in Results because it's essential to understanding the data

**Principle**: When a novel method is central to your findings, briefly justify its selection in Results.
